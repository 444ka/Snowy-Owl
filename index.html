The snowy owl is a...

Silent Hunter

Snowy Owl in flight!

The snowy owl's flight is characterized by its strength, directness, and near-silence, which is a key adaptation for stealth hunting in its open tundra habitat. They can reach speeds of up to 80 km/h.

Bright Yellow
Eyes

...perfect for spotting prey

Snowy owl eyesight is excellent, particularly for spotting prey from a distance. Their large, yellow eyes are highly sensitive to light and motion. Like other owls, their eyes are long and tubular, which prevents them from moving them, so they must turn their heads to look in different directions. This specialized vision means they are far-sighted and cannot focus well on close-up objects, but they use specialized bristles around their beaks to sense things up close.

Unlike most owls that are nocturnal,

snowy owls are...

Diurnal

Meaning active during the day

They live in the

Artic Tundra,

a vast, cold, treeless biome located in the Northern Hemisphere

Snowy owls can be found in the Arctic regions of North America, Europe, and Asia, where they breed on the tundra. In the winter, they migrate south to areas like southern Canada and the northern United States, and sometimes as far as the Caribbean, following food sources like lemmings.

Feathers
for feet!

Snowy owls have large feet that are entirely covered in dense, thick, white feathers, which serve as a crucial adaptation to their Arctic habitat. These unique "yeti boots" provide both insulation and functional benefits

Male snowy owls are typically smaller than females and are predominantly pure white

Female snowy owls are larger than males and have more brown or black barring and spots
